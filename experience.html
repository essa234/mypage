Nuffield Research Project

Keen to use my programming skills to learn more about AI through a practical experience, I completed a Nuffield research placement, where I worked alongside a group of 4 peers to assess how accurately machine learning could predict bankruptcy given linguistic variables about a company. Using data from thirty-five companies, I created and trained a neural network in MATLAB. I was excited to learn about hidden layers in a neural network, and how these are integral to the functioning of the system. Between input and output layers, hidden layers receive the input, attribute it a weight and feed it through an activation function, producing the output. The weight of each input signifies its influence on the output. In my neural network, this output was a prediction of whether a company would go bankrupt.
